Books I (Kate Bugos) Read in 2026

Last year I maintained a Twitter thread of all of the books I read. This year, given my attempt to spend less time on social media et cetera, I am going to keep a list here. I might write full reviews for some pieces, which I'll put on a blog somewhere on here, and others I might just list.

  1. After Geoengineering: Climate Tragedy, Repair, and Restoration

    by Holly Jean Buck

    Rating: 3.5/5


    I read this for Hothouse Bookclub. An interesting read that does a good job of untangling knee-jerk moralistic responses to geoengineering. More exploratory than solutions oriented which was a bit disappointing but fairly engaging writing for such a dense topic.
